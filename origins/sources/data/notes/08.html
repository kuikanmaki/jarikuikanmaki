Notes to Chapter 8

Note on Source 7

The European Commission’s 2020 guide “Identifying conspiracy theories” explicitly classified claims that SARS-CoV-2 was artificially created or resulted from laboratory manipulation as dangerous conspiracy theories. Later reporting revealed that Germany’s BND intelligence service had already assessed, in spring 2020, an 80–90% likelihood that the virus resulted from a laboratory accident involving gain-of-function research at the Wuhan Institute of Virology (see Chapter 19 Sources: Burger & Rinke 2025). This intelligence assessment remains classified. The Commission’s original guidance — which labeled such conclusions as dangerous conspiracy theories — remains online and unchanged on its website.

Beijing’s Influence on the EU

Contemporary reporting also suggested EU officials softened their language on Chinese disinformation under pressure from Beijing (a claim Brussels denied):

Apuzzo, Matt, Pressured by China, E.U. Softens Report on Covid-19 Disinformation, New York Times, April 24, 2020.

Notably, an archived version of the Wuhan Institute of Virology’s pre-pandemic website also listed the European Union as one of its official international partners.

Note on Source 16

In late April 2020, the U.S. Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI) stated that the intelligence community agreed with the scientific consensus that SARS-CoV-2 was “not manmade or genetically modified.” However, a later declassified Defense Intelligence Agency (DIA) genome analysis from June 2020 already appears to contradict or complicate that public assessment (see Chapter 19 Sources: DIA 2020 / USRTK FOIA release 2025).

Note on Source 19

The chapter references studies listed on the Project Evidence website. One of those studies, from 2019, describes experiments in which piglets were inoculated with a chimeric bat coronavirus. Another relevant study co-authored by Shi Zhengli is listed in Chapter 12 Sources (Zhou et al 2018).
