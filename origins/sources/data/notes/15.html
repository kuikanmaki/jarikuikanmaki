Notes to Chapter 15

Note on Source 5

Baric on Viral Genomics. In his 2006 paper on synthetic viral genomics, Ralph Baric warned of the significant dual-use risks of these technologies. He noted that synthetic genomes could be engineered to exactly mimic natural strains from any location or year, giving an attacker plausible deniability or the ability to deliberately misdirect investigations by leaving a genetic signature that points to a false origin.

Note on Source 6

AMMS, Dual-Use, and Genetic Weapons. The 2015 Chinese book, authored by 18 experts (16 of whom were serving officers at the Academy of Military Medical Sciences or other PLA research centers), stated:

“If one uses a modern genetic weapon, it will be stealthy and difficult to collect evidence; no matter if academic evidence is provided, or even empirical proof of the virus or the animal, there are still a hundred and one ways to deny this too, to block and suppress, and to leave international organizations and advocates for justice utterly helpless.”
“Modern genetic weapons are much more intelligent [than traditional bioweapons] and leave the targeted areas completely unsuspecting, unsuccessful in their prevention efforts, and even throw their thinking into a state of chaos in which they cannot understand the true situation. Even more horrible to contemplate, and which leaves people distressed, is the likelihood that some individuals will blindly believe that the conditions and motives do not currently exist to produce and release a ‘modern genetic weapon’, the result being that these people will unintentionally obstruct to one extent or another the search for the origin of the release of the genetic weapon.”
