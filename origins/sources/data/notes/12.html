Notes to Chapter 12

Note on Sources 1–3

Ralph Baric’s Research Trajectory. The chapter draws on several key papers by Ralph Baric that trace the evolution of his research, which later culminated in collaboration with Shi Zhengli and the Wuhan Institute of Virology:

  • Yount et al. (2002): An early study describing the creation of a synthetic coronavirus clone in a laboratory.
  • Roberts et al. (2007): Researchers created a new variant of SARS-CoV-1 through serial passage in laboratory mice.
  • Becker et al. (2008): The spike protein from SARS-CoV-1 was inserted into a synthetic coronavirus backbone.

Note on Sources 7–11

Shi Zhengli’s Research Trajectory. These peer-reviewed studies, co-authored by Shi Zhengli, document the development of key coronavirus research capabilities at the Wuhan Institute of Virology in the years leading up to the pandemic:

  • Li et al. (2005): Established horseshoe bats as the natural reservoir of SARS-like coronaviruses.
  • Ren et al. (2008): Demonstrated that bat SARS-like coronaviruses could be genetically modified to infect human cells using chimeric spike proteins.
  • Hou et al. (2010): Tested ACE2 receptors from different bat species to identify those allowing SARS-like viruses to enter human cells.
  • Zeng et al. (2016): Created a full infectious clone of a live bat coronavirus (WIV1) and generated gene-deleted mutants. The reverse-genetics platform developed in this study later appeared in a U.S. military intelligence analysis of SARS-CoV-2 origins (see Chapter 19 Sources: DIA 2020). The experiments were conducted under BSL-2 conditions (see Chapter 12 Sources: Jacobsen 2021 and Chapter 17 Notes on biosafety in Wuhan).
  • Hu et al. (2017): Isolated a new SARS-related bat coronavirus from a Yunnan cave and showed that both the native virus and chimeric versions could infect human cells.
  • Zhou et al. (2018): Infected piglets with a bat coronavirus isolated and studied at the Wuhan Institute of Virology.

Science Censorship in China

In the early weeks of the outbreak, Chinese authorities issued strict directives to control scientific research and publication. Key examples include these directives from the CCDC and the State Council, requiring central approval and pre-vetting for all COVID-19-related research:

  • Memorandum on the Supplementary Regulations on Strengthening the Management of Science and Technology During the Emergency Response to the Novel Coronavirus Pneumonia, Chinese Center for Disease Control and Prevention (CCDC), February 25, 2020.
  • Notice on the Standardization of the Management of Publication of Novel Coronavirus Pneumonia Scientific Research, The State Council Novel Coronavirus Pneumonia Epidemic Joint Prevention and Control Mechanism Scientific Research Task Force, March 3, 2020.

For additional reporting on these censorship measures, see:

  • Gan, Nectar, Hu, Caitlin, and Watson, Ivan, Beijing tightens grip over coronavirus research amid US-China row on virus origin, CNN, April 16, 2020.
  • Zhong, Raymond, et al., No 'Negative' News: How China Censored the Coronavirus, New York Times, December 19, 2020.

For the closure of the laboratory that first shared the SARS-CoV-2 genome sequence, see Chapter 8 Sources: Zhuang, 2020.

Rand Paul vs. Anthony Fauci: The Gain-of-Function Dispute

After Senator Rand Paul confronted Anthony Fauci in 2021 Senate hearings, several major fact-checking organizations sided with Fauci, treating the GoF dispute as definitional and accepting NIH’s denials. Examples include:

  • Robertson, Lori, The Wuhan Lab and the Gain-of-Function Disagreement, FactCheck.org, May 21, 2021.
  • Kessler, Glenn, Fact-checking Senator Paul and Dr. Fauci flap over Wuhan lab funding, The Washington Post, May 18, 2021.

Later revelations showed that Senator Paul was correct: Fauci and NIH had misled Congress and the public. The EcoHealth Alliance-funded experiments in Wuhan met every common definition of dangerous gain-of-function research. For more on this, see Chapter 16 Notes, Chapter 19, and the following sources:

  • Jacobsen, Rowan, How Dr. Fauci and Other Officials Withheld Information on China's Coronavirus Experiments, Newsweek, November 22, 2021.
  • Paul, Rand, Deception: The Great Covid Cover-Up, Regnery, October 10, 2023.
